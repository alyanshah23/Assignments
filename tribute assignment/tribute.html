Imran khan

Imran Khan, born on October 5, 1952, in Lahore, Pakistan, is one of the most prominent figures in the country’s history. Educated at Aitchison College and later at Oxford University, he first rose to fame as an exceptional cricketer. His talent, discipline, and leadership took Pakistan to its greatest sporting victory — winning the 1992 Cricket World Cup. This achievement made him a national hero and a symbol of pride for Pakistan. Known for his determination and charisma, Imran’s success in cricket laid the foundation for his later roles as a philanthropist and political leader.

After retiring from cricket, Imran Khan devoted himself to social work and philanthropy. Inspired by his mother’s battle with cancer, he established the Shaukat Khanum Memorial Cancer Hospital in 1994, providing free or affordable treatment to thousands of patients. He also founded Namal University to offer higher education opportunities to underprivileged students. These projects reflected his deep concern for the poor and his belief in using his fame for public good. Through his efforts, Imran earned widespread respect as a humanitarian dedicated to improving lives in Pakistan.

In 1996, Imran entered politics by founding the Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf (PTI) party, with the goal of fighting corruption and promoting justice. Although his political journey was slow and filled with challenges, his message of change eventually gained strong public support. In 2018, PTI won the national elections, and Imran Khan became Pakistan’s 22nd Prime Minister. During his tenure, he focused on welfare programs, poverty reduction, and building a self-reliant economy. Despite political controversies and his removal from office in 2022, Imran Khan remains one of Pakistan’s most influential leaders — a man who made a lasting mark in sports, charity, and politics.
